05. 細爆裂音

Fine Crackles

聽診音檔Auscultation Audio
音檔改作自公開CC BY授權教學影片，詳細來源見「關於」頁。
Adapted from openly licensed (CC BY) educational recordings — full source details on the About page.

細碎、不連續的爆裂聲，好發於吸氣中後段，是肺纖維化等疾病的重要聽診線索。

Fine, discontinuous crackling sounds occurring mainly in mid-to-late inspiration — an important auscultatory clue for pulmonary fibrosis and related conditions.

🩺 聽診位置🩺 Auscultation Location

依病灶而異，常見於雙側肺底（dependent肺野）。

Location varies with the underlying pathology; commonly heard in the dependent (basal) lung fields.

📊 聲學特性📊 Acoustic Characteristics

音調Pitch柔和、高音調Soft, high-pitched
頻率Frequency約650 HzApprox. 650 Hz
時長／連續性Duration / Continuity極短暫，每次約5–10毫秒；好發吸氣中後段（mid-late inspiration）Very brief, approx. 5–10 ms per crackle; occurs mainly in mid-to-late inspiration

⚙️ 產生機轉⚙️ Mechanism

兩派理論並陳：①氣道內分泌物被氣流衝擊產生氣泡音；②塌陷的小氣道在吸氣時突然彈開所致（後者較能解釋為何好發於吸氣中後段）。研究顯示：聲音出現得越晚，代表開啟氣道所需張力越大，此現象在限制性肺疾病（restrictive lung disease）比肺泡填滿型疾病更常見。

Two mechanisms are proposed: (1) gas bubbling through airway secretions; (2) sudden opening of small airways that had collapsed, held closed by surface tension — the latter better explains the mid-to-late inspiratory timing. Later-occurring crackles imply greater airway-opening pressure is required, a pattern more typical of restrictive lung disease than alveolar-filling processes.

🔍 臨床意義🔍 Clinical Significance

又稱「Velcro rales」（如撕開魔鬼氈的聲音）。常見於肺纖維化（interstitial fibrosis）、肺炎恢復期（resolving pneumonia）、肺不張（atelectasis）、左心衰竭。

Also called "Velcro rales" for their resemblance to tearing open Velcro. Commonly associated with pulmonary fibrosis, resolving pneumonia, atelectasis, and left-sided heart failure.
