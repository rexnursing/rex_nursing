03. 支氣管肺泡音

Bronchovesicular Breath Sounds

聽診音檔Auscultation Audio

Adapted from openly licensed (CC BY) educational recordings — full source details on the About page.

介於肺泡音與支氣管音之間的「中間音」，聽診於大氣道經過、但尚未完全分散到肺野的區域。

An intermediate sound between vesicular and bronchial breath sounds, heard over regions where large airways pass but sound has not yet fully dispersed into the lung periphery.

🩺 聽診位置🩺 Auscultation Location

前胸第1–2肋間、兩側肩胛骨之間；6歲以下兒童因胸壁較薄，外側肺野也可能聽到（屬正常兒科差異）。

1st–2nd intercostal spaces anteriorly, and between the scapulae posteriorly. In children under 6, thinner chest walls mean it may also be heard more peripherally — a normal pediatric variant, not a pathological finding.

📊 聲學特性📊 Acoustic Characteristics

音調Pitch音調偏低、介於肺泡音與支氣管音之間Relatively low pitch, intermediate between vesicular and bronchial sounds
頻率Frequency未見獨立Hz數據（文獻以性質描述為主，介於肺泡音200–600Hz與支氣管音之間）No independently reported Hz range in the source texts; described qualitatively as intermediate between vesicular (200–600 Hz) and bronchial sounds
時長／連續性Duration / Continuity吸吐氣時長接近1:1，有時中間可聽到短暫靜默間隔Inspiration and expiration roughly equal in length (approx. 1:1), sometimes with a brief silent gap between the two

⚙️ 產生機轉⚙️ Mechanism

與肺泡音、支氣管音同源，皆為氣流通過氣道的渦流亂流，差別在於聽診位置介於兩者之間，聲音因而混合了兩種特性。

Shares the same turbulent-airflow origin as vesicular and bronchial sounds; the intermediate location produces a blended acoustic character.

🔍 臨床意義🔍 Clinical Significance

若在成人的周邊肺野（原本該聽到肺泡音之處）聽到此中間音，需提高警覺，可能代表該處肺組織已被實變（consolidation）取代。

Hearing this intermediate sound in the lung periphery of an adult (where vesicular sounds are normally expected) should raise concern for underlying consolidation.

📌 2022年版《Murray & Nadel's》全書未使用「Bronchovesicular」這個分類，僅用Normal／Tracheal／Bronchial三分類。多數文獻與現行教學仍支持四分類架構，本站維持四分類，僅在此註記此一命名學趨勢。 📌 The 2022 edition of Murray & Nadel's does not use "bronchovesicular" as a separate category at all, classifying sounds only as Normal / Tracheal / Bronchial. Most literature and current teaching still support the four-category framework, which this site retains — this note is included purely as a point of interest.
